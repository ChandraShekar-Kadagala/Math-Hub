UNIT 1 - MATRICES

Q8. Find the reduced row echelon (RREF) form and deduce the rank of the following matrices:

RREF & Rank

(a) \[ \begin{pmatrix} 1 & -1 & 2 \\ 3 & 0 & 2 \\ 1 & -3 & -1 \end{pmatrix} \]

Applying row operations: \]R_2 \to R_2 - 3R_1$, $R_3 \to R_3 - R_1$. Then normalizing pivot rows. All 3 rows will be linearly independent.

✅ RREF is the Identity Matrix \[I_3\[. Rank = 3.

(b) \[ \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 3 & -2 & 4 \\ 0 & -2 & 0 & 4 \\ 4 & 1 & 3 & 0 \\ 1 & 2 & -1 & -3 \end{pmatrix} \]

Reducing this \]4 \times 4$ matrix through standard Gaussian elimination yields 4 non-zero pivot rows.

✅ RREF is the Identity Matrix \[I_4\[. Rank = 4.

(c) \[ \begin{pmatrix} 3 & -3 & -2 & -1 \\ 0 & 2 & -3 & -3 \\ 3 & 3 & 2 & -3 \end{pmatrix} \]

\]R_3 \to R_3 - R_1\[ eliminates the first column in row 3. Continuing to RREF leaves 3 pivots.

✅ Rank = 3.

(d) \[ \begin{pmatrix} -1 & 1 & -1 & -2 \\ 2 & 0 & 0 & 3 \\ -1 & 0 & -1 & -2 \\ -3 & 0 & 2 & -1 \end{pmatrix} \]

✅ Rank = 4. RREF is \]I_4$.

Q9. Find three $2 \times 2$ matrices A, B, C such that $AB=AC$ with $B \neq C$ and \[A \neq 0\[.

Matrix Properties

We need A to be a non-invertible matrix (zero divisor). Let \[ A = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 \end{pmatrix} \]

Let \[ B = \begin{pmatrix} 0 & 0 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix}, \quad C = \begin{pmatrix} 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{pmatrix} \]. Note \]B \neq C\[.

Then \[ AB = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 \end{pmatrix}\begin{pmatrix} 0 & 0 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 \end{pmatrix} \]

And \[ AC = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 \end{pmatrix}\begin{pmatrix} 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 \end{pmatrix} \]

✅ A, B, C chosen such that \]AB=AC=0$ despite $B \neq C$. This demonstrates matrix multiplication lacks the cancellation property when $A$ is not invertible.

Q10. Let A be an $n \times n$ upper triangular matrix. Determine which statements hold true:

Invertibility

(a) If $A \neq 0$, then A is invertible.
False. A can be non-zero but still have a zero on the diagonal (e.g., $a_{11}=1$, $a_{22}=0$). Since determinant is the product of diagonals, $\det(A) = 0$, making it not invertible.

(b) If $A \neq 0$, then A is not invertible.
False. If all diagonal entries are non-zero, then $\det(A) \neq 0$, so A IS invertible.

(c) If $A \neq 0$ with $a_{ii} = 0$ for all $i$, then $A^n = 0$.
True. An upper triangular matrix with zeros on the main diagonal is strictly upper triangular. Such a matrix is nilpotent, and specifically $A^n = 0\[.

Q11. Let \[A = \begin{pmatrix} \cos\theta & \sin\theta \\ -\sin\theta & \cos\theta \end{pmatrix}\]. Find an expression for $A^k$.

Rotation Matrix

$A$ represents a clockwise rotation by $\theta$. Multiplying \[A\[ by itself adds the angles of rotation.

\[ A^2 = \begin{pmatrix} \cos\theta & \sin\theta \\ -\sin\theta & \cos\theta \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} \cos\theta & \sin\theta \\ -\sin\theta & \cos\theta \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} \cos 2\theta & \sin 2\theta \\ -\sin 2\theta & \cos 2\theta \end{pmatrix} \]

By mathematical induction, applying it \]k$ times rotates by \[k\theta\[.

✅ \[ A^k = \begin{pmatrix} \cos(k\theta) & \sin(k\theta) \\ -\sin(k\theta) & \cos(k\theta) \end{pmatrix} \]

Q18. Let A be a \]3 \times 3$ matrix with eigenvalues -3, 4, 4 and eigenvectors $v_1, v_2, v_3$. Find $D$ and $P$ without computation.

Diagonalization

A matrix $A$ is diagonalizable if there exists an invertible matrix $P$ and a diagonal matrix $D$ such that $P^{-1}AP = D$.

The diagonal matrix \[D\[ is simply the matrix with the eigenvalues on the main diagonal.

\[ D = \begin{pmatrix} -3 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 4 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 4 \end{pmatrix} \]

The matrix \]P$ is formed by placing the corresponding eigenvectors as columns. Since the exact vectors $v_1, v_2, v_3\[ are partially obscured in the raw text, we write the general form:

✅ \[ P = \begin{pmatrix} | & | & | \\ v_1 & v_2 & v_3 \\ | & | & | \end{pmatrix} \]
