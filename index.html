🎯 Exam Strategy — How to Score 100%
Step 1 — Identify Type

Before solving, classify the equation. This alone earns marks even if your arithmetic fails. Practice this in the first 30 seconds of reading any question.

Step 2 — Write the Formula

Always write the formula you're using: Integrating Factor, Characteristic Equation, Wronskian, Rank condition. Examiners award method marks separately.

Step 3 — Step-by-Step Work

Never skip steps. Write each row operation, each integration clearly. Box your final answer. An examiner should follow your working with zero ambiguity.

Step 4 — Apply ICs Last

Find the general solution first, then substitute the initial condition. Never forget +C after integration.

Step 5 — Unit 1 & 2 Tips

For rank: always reduce to REF. For eigenvalues: always compute det(A−λI)=0 first. For diagonalization: list eigenvectors as columns of P.

Step 6 — Common Errors

Forgetting +C · Wrong sign in P(x) · Not checking exactness · Using eˣ instead of xᵐ in Euler-Cauchy · Modification rule for Undetermined Coefficients.
